About

Researcher, builder, and believer in frontier-scale AI.

I am a PhD student at the School of Computer Science, Peking University. My research spans scientific intelligence, multimodal learning, world modeling, embodied intelligence, and multi-agent systems.

The target is not incremental convenience. I care about systems that can discover new physical mechanisms, accelerate materials innovation, support automated theorem proving, and attack hard scientific conjectures.

I am equally interested in AI-native production for film, games, and interactive worlds, as well as general embodied intelligence that can address structural labor shortages in the physical world.

Featured Project

One project that captures how I think about AI systems.

celebrity-studio-for-anything

Multi-agent deliberation with historical and contemporary figures.

This project lets multiple well-known figures question, support, supplement, advance, and converge around the same problem, then produce a conclusion that is closer to a real decision process than a one-shot answer.

  • Structured challenge, support, and synthesis between agents
  • Designed for executable conclusions instead of shallow consensus
  • Fits practical reasoning, creative exploration, and strategic analysis
